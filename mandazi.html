Mandazi (East African fried dough)

Description

Mandazi, an East African pastry, entices with its sweet, spiced allure. These fried dough treats, popular in Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda, are made from a blend of flour, sugar, and coconut milk or milk. The dough, infused with fragrant cardamom, is fried to a golden crispness, revealing a soft interior.

Often paired with tea, mandazi offers a delightful contrast of textures and flavors—a culinary delight that bridges tradition and indulgence, reflecting the region's rich cultural and gastronomic heritage.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom (optional, for flavor)
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk or regular milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or oil
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients

    In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, ground cardamom (if using), and a pinch of salt. Mix well to evenly distribute the dry ingredients.
  2. Combine Wet Ingredients

    In a separate bowl, mix together the coconut milk or regular milk, beaten egg, and melted butter or oil.
  3. Create Dough

    Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Mix to form a dough. If the dough is too sticky, you can add a bit more flour. Knead the dough until it's smooth and pliable.
  4. Let the Dough Rest

    Cover the dough with a clean cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for about 30 minutes. This helps the dough relax and makes it easier to work with.
  5. Roll and Cut

    On a floured surface, roll out the rested dough to about 1/2-inch thickness.

    Use a knife to cut the dough into desired shapes—commonly triangles or rectangles
  6. Heat Oil

    Heat oil in a deep frying pan or pot to about 350-375°F (175-190°C).
  7. Fry Mandazi

    Carefully slide a few pieces of cut dough into the hot oil. Be cautious not to overcrowd the pan.

    Fry the mandazi until they are golden brown on both sides, turning as needed. This should take about 2-3 minutes per side.
  8. Fry Mandazi

    Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried mandazi from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

    Once the mandazi have cooled slightly, they are ready to be enjoyed. They can be served warm or at room temperature.

    If you prefer a sweeter touch, you can dust the mandazi with powdered sugar before serving.
